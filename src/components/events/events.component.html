Understanding Ekadasi

What is Ekadasi?

Ekadasi is a significant day observed by Hindus, dedicated to Lord Vishnu. It occurs twice a month, on the 11th day of each lunar fortnight in the Hindu calendar. The day is marked by fasting and spiritual practices.

Significance of Ekadasi

Ekadasi is considered an auspicious day for spiritual growth and self-discipline. Observing Ekadasi is believed to bring peace, prosperity, and liberation from the cycle of birth and death.

  • Spiritual Benefits: It is a day for deep meditation and reflection.
  • Health Benefits: Fasting can detoxify the body and improve health.
  • Cultural Importance: It strengthens devotion and religious practice.

Rituals and Practices

On Ekadasi, devotees observe fasting which may involve consuming only fruits and milk or completely abstaining from food and water. Special prayers and rituals are performed to honor Lord Vishnu.

  1. Perform morning rituals and prayers.
  2. Attend temple services or engage in devotional singing and chanting.
  3. Break the fast the following day after sunrise with a simple meal.
